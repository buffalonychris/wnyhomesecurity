## 1. Purpose

This document creates a unified WNY Home Security claims guardrail for future public copy, solution pages, package pages, ads, sales language, proposals, internal quote notes, field documentation, and customer-facing explanations.

It converts the current WNYHS catalog and hardware governance posture into a reusable claims standard so future work does not drift into unsupported, guarantee-based, life-safety, medical, emergency-response, professional-monitoring, or device-capability claims.

- Public claims must match approved solution status, hardware status, and field-tested capability.

## 3. Universal Claims Rule

Every WNYHS claim must pass all four tests:

| Test | Required Question |
|---|---|
| **Solution Test** | Is the claim tied to a governed WNYHS solution or approved package concept? |
| **Hardware Test** | Is the claim supported by Approved Standard, Conditional, Pilot, Premium/Custom, or Research-Only hardware status? |
| **Scope Test** | Does the claim describe selected monitored areas, supported devices, and site-reviewed conditions instead of universal outcomes? |
| **Risk Test** | Does the claim avoid guarantees, emergency response, medical monitoring, professional monitoring, life-safety replacement, crime prevention, and damage prevention? |

If any test fails, the claim must be rewritten or blocked.

- customer-owned equipment

- professionally installed smart property systems

- site-reviewed custom options
- compatibility-reviewed add-ons
- practical routines
- entry awareness

## 6. Category-Specific Claims Guardrails

### 6.1 Home Security Claims

Safe direction:

- front-door awareness
- package-area awareness
- entry/perimeter awareness
- supported camera visibility
- garage status awareness
- door/window open/closed awareness
- lock status and access-code management where supported
- selected notifications
- local dashboard visibility

Do not claim:

- crime prevention
- intruder prevention
- guaranteed deterrence
- police dispatch
- professional monitoring
- package-theft prevention
- guaranteed event capture
- guaranteed vehicle/person detection
- replacement for a monitored alarm system

Required framing:

> WNYHS Home Security solutions are awareness, visibility, access, and practical alert systems unless a separate monitored-alarm service is explicitly documented. Standard WNYHS catalog language must not imply professional monitoring, dispatch, or guaranteed prevention.

---

### 6.2 Home Safety / Environmental Safety Claims

Safe direction:

- water leak awareness in selected areas
- freeze-risk awareness in monitored spaces
- basement and utility room visibility
- humidity and moisture awareness
- sump-area water presence awareness
- outage/freezer awareness only where backup-power and sensor path are validated
- custom water shutoff only after site/valve validation
- secondary smoke/CO awareness only as research/custom, never as replacement alarms

Do not claim:

- water-damage prevention
- leak prevention
- frozen-pipe prevention
- mold prevention
- guaranteed sump-failure detection
- guaranteed outage alerting
- food/freezer protection
- smoke/CO monitoring
- replacement for code-required alarms
- universal automatic shutoff compatibility

Required framing:

> Environmental solutions provide earlier awareness and selected-area visibility. They do not eliminate the underlying property risk and do not replace maintenance, plumbing, HVAC, electrical, insurance, smoke/CO alarms, or emergency services.

---

### 6.3 Home Lighting Claims

Safe direction:

- everyday lighting convenience
- entry lighting automation
- night path lighting
- stair/hall/bathroom route lighting
- exterior lighting behavior on eligible circuits
- vacation lighting routines
- scene buttons and local controls
- garage/workshop lighting automation

Do not claim:

- crime prevention
- fall prevention
- guaranteed security improvement
- guaranteed energy savings
- universal circuit compatibility
- compatibility with every switch box, wiring condition, fixture, neutral configuration, exterior circuit, or landscape transformer

Required framing:

> Lighting solutions improve convenience, consistency, visibility, and routines where circuits and hardware are compatible. They do not guarantee crime prevention, fall prevention, energy savings, or universal electrical compatibility.

---

### 6.4 Home Automation Claims

Safe direction:

- one dashboard for supported devices
- cross-brand unification where integrations allow it
- away/night/vacation modes
- notification routing
- scene controls
- smart plug routines for rated plug-in loads
- thermostat setback only after compatibility review
- whole-home status dashboards as premium scope

Do not claim:

- every device can be unified
- every brand works locally
- every device avoids cloud dependency
- every automation works without internet
- guaranteed energy savings
- guaranteed HVAC performance
- safe control of high-risk appliances
- large appliance control unless specifically rated and approved

Required framing:

> Automation claims must describe supported integrations and reviewed devices, not universal smart-home compatibility.

---

### 6.5 Elder Care / Aging-in-Place Claims

Safe direction:

- non-medical home awareness
- entry/exit awareness
- fixed in-home help/assistance trigger
- family check-in dashboard
- comfort/temperature awareness
- night path lighting
- selected notifications
- routine signals only where disclosed and consent-aware

Do not claim:

- medical monitoring
- fall detection
- emergency response
- caregiver replacement
- guaranteed elder safety
- guaranteed detection of inactivity, distress, wandering, or falls
- 911 replacement
- nursing/care service replacement

Required framing:

> WNYHS aging-in-place solutions are non-medical awareness and convenience systems. They may help selected family or household members stay informed, but they do not provide medical monitoring, fall detection, emergency response, or caregiver replacement.

## 7. Hardware-Status Claims Rules

Claims must align with hardware registry status.

| Hardware Status | Allowed Claim Posture |
|---|---|
| **Approved Standard** | May support standard solution claims within documented scope after copy/BOM/SOP approval. |
| **Conditional** | Must include compatibility, ecosystem, app/cloud, or site-review language where relevant. |
| **Pilot** | Must not be marketed as standard public hardware. Internal, pilot, or custom language only. |
| **Premium / Custom** | Must be framed as site-reviewed, custom, or premium scope. No flat public guarantee language. |
| **Research-Only** | Must not appear as active public offer. Research/reference only. |
| **Excluded** | Must not be positioned as WNYHS standard. Exception only as custom/pass-through if separately allowed and disclosed. |

No hardware status allows unsupported claims. Even Approved Standard hardware only supports claims within selected installation scope and documented capability.

## 8. Required Disclosure Triggers

The following situations require disclosure before public copy, proposal language, or customer agreement language is approved.

| Trigger | Required Disclosure |
|---|---|
| Conditional device | Explain compatibility or ecosystem dependency. |
| Cloud/vendor app dependency | State that some setup, features, remote access, or operation may rely on vendor app/cloud. |
| Subscription-optional or subscription-dependent hardware | State what does and does not require a subscription. |
| Camera or doorbell recording | Explain privacy, audio, recording, retention, field of view, and placement limits. |
| Smart lock/access code use | Explain customer responsibility for codes, access permissions, door prep, and mechanical key backup. |
| Garage control | Separate status awareness from active control; active control requires compatibility review. |
| Water shutoff | State compatible valve/site requirements and non-guarantee of damage prevention. |
| Smoke/CO secondary awareness | State it does not replace smoke/CO alarms and is not life-safety monitoring. |
| Elder/family alerts | State non-medical, non-emergency nature; define recipients and quiet-hours rules. |
| Thermostat/HVAC | State compatibility review and no energy/HVAC-performance guarantee. |
| Outdoor/exterior electrical | State site/circuit/weatherproofing review requirements. |
| Outage/freezer alerts | State backup power, sensor path, network, and notification dependencies. |
| Pilot hardware | State pilot/custom nature and support boundaries. |

## 9. Public Copy Review Checklist

Before any public WNYHS copy is deployed, the operator or reviewer must check:

1. Does the copy avoid forbidden claim types?
2. Is every claim tied to an approved or conditionally approved solution?
3. Is every hardware-specific claim supported by hardware status?
4. Does the copy avoid universal language such as “always,” “guaranteed,” “every,” “never,” “all,” “complete protection,” or “prevents”?
5. Does the copy use awareness/control/convenience language?
6. Does the copy disclose custom, conditional, cloud, subscription, or site-review boundaries where needed?
7. Does the copy avoid professional-monitoring, dispatch, emergency-response, medical, fall-detection, and caregiver-replacement implications?
8. Does “no required monthly fees” apply only to the supported WNYHS Core/local path being described?
9. Does “local-first” apply only where selected hardware supports that path?
10. Does the copy avoid claiming code compliance, insurance approval, or life-safety replacement unless separately documented?

If any answer fails, the copy must be rewritten before deployment.

## 10. Advertising and Social Claims Rules

Ads, flyers, QR placards, social posts, printed handouts, direct mail, and paid search copy must obey the same claims rules as the website.

Short ad copy is especially risky because it compresses context. Use compact but safe phrases.

Preferred ad phrase patterns:

- “See more of what happens at your front door.”
- “Get practical alerts for selected areas of your home.”
- “Know sooner when water shows up where it should not.”
- “Make everyday lighting easier to manage.”
- “Help family stay informed with non-medical home awareness.”
- “Customer-owned smart property systems, professionally installed.”
- “No required monthly fees for WNYHS Core/local automations where supported.”
- “Local-first smart property systems where supported hardware allows it.”

Avoid ad phrase patterns:

- “Stop package theft.”
- “Prevent break-ins.”
- “Never worry about water damage again.”
- “Protect your elderly parent.”
- “Fall detection without monthly fees.”
- “Guaranteed garage security.”
- “No cloud ever.”
- “Works with everything.”
- “Replaces your alarm company.”
- “Emergency alerts to save your home.”

## 11. Quote, Proposal, and Sales-Language Rules

Internal sales and proposal language must remain claim-safe.

Allowed:

- describe selected hardware
- describe selected monitored areas
- describe alerts and dashboard views
- describe installation assumptions
- describe customer responsibilities
- describe compatibility review
- describe custom boundaries
- describe known dependencies
- describe add-on paths

Not allowed:

- overselling coverage
- implying guaranteed prevention
- implying professional monitoring
- implying emergency dispatch
- implying medical monitoring
- implying universal compatibility
- hiding cloud/app/subscription dependencies
- hiding customer-owned-equipment or Core/add-on pricing boundaries
- implying WNYHS is responsible for third-party cloud/service changes unless separately contracted
