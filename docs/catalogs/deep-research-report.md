# WNYHS Capability Catalog Technical Validation REV03

## Executive Summary

This validation uses the attached `wnyhs_capability_catalog_rev02.md` as the authoritative feature list and keeps every original feature row intact. The attached module roadmap was used as the controlling strategic frame: WNYHS Core is the local control layer, customer ownership and local operation are preferred, required vendor clouds and subscriptions should be avoided, and pricing should follow technical validation rather than drive it. fileciteturn0file0 fileciteturn0file1

The highest-confidence technical pattern for first launch is a narrow, local-first stack built around Home Assistant, Z-Wave or Zigbee sensors, PoE cameras where cameras are needed, local recording, and carefully chosen integrations with strong local characteristics such as Z-Wave JS, ZHA, MQTT, Shelly, Lutron Caséta, WLED, IoTaWatt, Venstar local API, Ecowitt local push, AirGradient local polling, Airthings BLE, Reolink local integration, and Frigate for local NVR/object detection. Home Assistant documents these integrations as local or local-push/local-polling in many of the relevant cases, while Frigate explicitly positions itself as a complete local NVR for Home Assistant and recommends H.264/AAC and multiple streams for best results. citeturn2view0turn2view1turn2view2turn3view2turn25view0turn30view1turn31view0turn32view0turn32view1turn35view0turn3view4turn4view0turn45view0

The strongest first-launch candidates are leak detection, basement flood alerts, freeze alerts, local recording, front-door awareness, smart lighting, motion lighting, scheduled lighting, home/away/night modes, garage door control for supported openers, and simple camera dashboards. These all align with repeatable installation patterns, clear homeowner pain points, and relatively strong local-control paths. By contrast, fall detection, water quality monitoring, pool chemistry monitoring, advanced guest-code workflows, severe-weather alerting beyond basic integrations, and many vehicle/RV/boat use cases remain research-only or custom-quote due to weak local-only paths, support risk, or unclear hardware standards. citeturn3view4turn4view0turn29view0turn39view0turn19view1turn20view1turn30view1turn31view0turn35view0turn37view0turn37view2turn12view0

A strict reading of “local-only” changes several ratings. Phone push notifications are not automatically “fully local,” because Home Assistant’s local push only works on the internal URL and internal network; off-site or standard mobile push paths can involve Apple or Google push infrastructure. Ecobee remains cloud-account based in its official integration. Jandy iAquaLink is explicitly cloud-account based. Ecowitt is local push, but requires HTTP callback configuration and cannot use HTTPS-only Home Assistant endpoints. Two-way door communication is possible with certain video doorbells, but the cleanest end-user path still often depends on vendor app workflows and therefore needs bench validation before any package-level claim. citeturn12view0turn31view1turn37view2turn35view0turn15view0turn3view4

## Research Basis

The validation logic below follows a simple rule set. When a feature can be delivered by a documented local Home Assistant integration with stable device classes and a clear support path, it was rated more favorably. When delivery appears to rely on cloud accounts, vendor apps for routine use, fragile HACS-only workflows, or custom Node-RED logic with high maintenance burden, the rating was pushed toward later package, custom quote, research only, or not recommended. HACS is useful, but it adds install/update overhead and should be treated as a deliberate support choice rather than a default. Node-RED is powerful low-code tooling, but it also adds another runtime and maintenance surface; in this catalog it is best reserved for edge cases rather than baseline packages. citeturn5view0turn8view0

Home Assistant dashboards, helpers, to-do entities, companion notifications, local voice, and local device integrations make it possible to build a local-first control plane for many “digital workflow” features in the catalog. Dashboards are drag-and-drop and family-friendly, `input_boolean`, `input_button`, and `input_datetime` support local helper/state logic, and local to-do entities can support checklist-style flows. Assist can run fully on local hardware, but voice remains a tuning-heavy subsystem and should be treated as later-stage until on-site performance is validated. citeturn9view0turn9view1turn9view2turn9view3turn9view4turn5view2

Open questions remain where the ecosystem is real but not yet clean enough for package standardization. Those include fall detection, robust glass-break without alarm-panel assumptions, pool/spa equipment outside local Pentair ScreenLogic cases, persistent vehicle/RV/boat monitoring away from the property, water-quality instrumentation, and some fine-grained access-code management workflows for rental properties. In those areas, this report intentionally uses “Research Required,” “Validation Required,” or conservative launch ratings rather than implying production readiness. That approach matches the roadmap instruction not to publish hard promises before bench validation. fileciteturn0file1

The best first-launch set is the one that keeps WNYHS Core focused on local control, clear homeowner value, and low support entropy. The highest-confidence first-launch candidates are: Leak Detection, Basement Flood Alerts, Freeze Alerts, Water Heater Monitoring, Utility Room Monitoring, Front Door Monitoring, Door Open Alerts, Window Open Alerts, Motion Detection, Local Recording, Multi-Camera Dashboard, Smart Lighting, Motion Lighting, Scheduled Lighting, Home Mode, Away Mode, Night Mode, Garage Door Control for supported openers, Nighttime Movement Lighting, Humidity Monitoring, Motion Security Lighting, Freezer Monitoring, and device-health software layers. ## Risk Notes and Compliance Boundaries

Several rows in this catalog carry elevated claims risk even when the technical path is real. Smoke Detector Integration, CO Detector Integration, Emergency Notification System, Fall Detection, Stove/Oven Left-On Alerts, severe-weather flows, and any caregiver-escalation workflow should be described internally as supplemental awareness and automation features unless and until there is specific certified hardware, compliance review, and bench-tested operating procedure to support stronger language. The roadmap’s instruction to avoid hard promises before bench testing should govern all of these rows. fileciteturn0file1

Camera rows should not imply guaranteed deterrence, identification, or incident prevention. Frigate and camera integrations are strong local tooling, but accuracy depends on placement, lighting, substream choices, and device load. ONVIF’s H.264 expectations and Frigate’s own hardware guidance make it clear that setup details materially affect outcome quality. That means customer-facing claims should stay outcome-oriented and conditional until the chosen camera families and mounting standards are validated in WNYHS field conditions. citeturn3view3turn45view0turn15view0turn16view1

Energy and HVAC rows should avoid guaranteed savings claims. IoTaWatt, Shelly-class energy monitoring, and Venstar local thermostats can provide excellent visibility and automation, but “efficiency alerts” and “savings” language should remain internal until actual rule sets, baselines, and exception logic are tested. Likewise, rental occupancy awareness, guest-code workflows, and caregiver dashboards should be handled as operational tooling, not compliance tooling, unless a separate legal/compliance review is completed. citeturn30view1turn31view0

Open questions remain concentrated in six areas: standardized panic-button hardware, fall detection, glass break, pool chemistry/water quality, robust lock-code lifecycle tooling, and parked-vehicle monitoring. Those rows were intentionally held back because the technical path is either not yet evidence-backed enough for repeatable deployment or creates support/compliance risk disproportionate to launch value.
